**Key clarifications:**
- **WWII peak — the "right" figure:** *Gross* federal debt peaked at **~119% of GDP in 1946**; *debt held by the public* peaked at **~106% in 1946**. Cite whichever measure your chart uses and label it. Sources: [Committee for a Responsible Federal Budget / Fraser Institute confirm ~119% gross 1946](https://www.fraserinstitute.org/commentary/wartime-presidents-added-most-us-federal-debt-gdp-ratio); the 106%→23% (1946→1974) path is the *debt-held-by-public* series ([IMF WP 2024/005](https://www.imf.org/-/media/Files/Publications/WP/2024/English/wpiea2024005-print-pdf.ashx); [CEPR](https://cepr.org/voxeu/columns/reassessing-fall-us-public-debt-after-world-war-ii)).
- **Postwar decline was not just growth:** IMF research finds the fall from 106% (1946) to 23% (1974) was driven mostly by primary budget surpluses, surprise inflation, and financial repression — not real GDP growth alone; without those, debt/GDP would have fallen only to ~74% by 1974. [IMF WP 2024/005](https://www.imf.org/-/media/Files/Publications/WP/2024/English/wpiea2024005-print-pdf.ashx)
- **Reagan tripling:** gross debt went from **$997.9B (1981) to ~$2.9T (1989)**, ~+186%. [24/7 Wall St.](https://247wallst.com/economy/2025/03/12/this-republican-president-nearly-tripled-the-u-s-debt-during-his-time-in-office/)
- **1790 Hamilton:** Hamilton calculated ~$75M in foreign+domestic debt in 1789; total ~$79M, ~40% of GDP. [Constituting America](https://constitutingamerica.org/august-4-1790-alexander-hamiltons-debt-plan-and-the-foundation-for-american-capitalism/) · [Battlefield Trust — Compromise of 1790](https://www.battlefields.org/learn/articles/compromise-1790)
- **1998–2001 surpluses (the last surpluses):** FY1998 **$69B**, FY1999 **$124B**, FY2000 **$236.2B** (record), FY2001 **$128B**. [Wikipedia — 2000/2001 federal budget](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000_United_States_federal_budget) · [Clinton White House archive](https://clintonwhitehouse3.archives.gov/WH/New/html/20000204_1.html)

**The acceleration story (the headline):**
- $1T → took ~200 years (1789→1981).
- Recent trillions arrive every **~2–5 months**. $37T (Aug 2025) → $38T (Oct 2025) was **~2 months**, the fastest ever outside COVID. [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/u-s-hits-38-trillion-in-debt-after-the-fastest-accumulation-of-1-trillion-outside-of-the-pandemic)
- Joint Economic Committee estimate: debt grew **$69,713.82 per second** over the trailing year. [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/u-s-hits-38-trillion-in-debt-after-the-fastest-accumulation-of-1-trillion-outside-of-the-pandemic)

## 3. Interest Burden (the crossover year)

- **Net interest FY2025:** ~**$970 billion** (net interest); some trackers cite ~$1.2T on a gross-interest basis. Interest surpassed $1T outlay-basis for the first time. [CRFB — Interest Costs Just Surpassed Defense and Medicare](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/interest-costs-just-surpassed-defense-and-medicare) · [Taxpayers for Common Sense](https://www.taxpayer.net/national-security/its-official-interest-payments-on-national-debt-leapfrog-military-spending/)
- **Defense crossover:** **FY2025 is the crossover** — net interest (~$970B) exceeded defense (~$917B) by ~$100–150B. Interest is now the **3rd-largest** line item, having passed both **defense and Medicare**. CBO projects interest > defense outlays every year 2025–2035. [CRFB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/interest-costs-just-surpassed-defense-and-medicare) · [Taxpayers for Common Sense](https://www.taxpayer.net/national-security/its-official-interest-payments-on-national-debt-leapfrog-military-spending/)
- **Interest as % of federal revenue:** ~**18.5–19%** in FY2025 — exceeds the prior high set in 1991. [Econofact — Interest Burden of the Federal Debt](https://econofact.org/explainer/the-interest-burden-of-the-federal-debt)
- **Interest as % of total outlays:** ~**14%**. [CRFB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/interest-costs-just-surpassed-defense-and-medicare)
- **Average rate on the debt — now vs 2021:** average rate on interest-bearing debt hit a 20-year low of **~1.6% in late 2021**; weighted-average rate on marketable Treasury debt is now **~3.34–3.35%** (2025/early 2026) — roughly **doubled**. Primary series: [Treasury Fiscal Data — Average Interest Rates on Treasury Securities](https://fiscaldata.treasury.gov/datasets/average-interest-rates-treasury-securities/) · [Econofact — Cost of Financing US Govt Debt](https://econofact.org/the-cost-of-financing-u-s-government-debt)
- **Weighted average maturity:** ~**5 years** long-run average (since 1980); ~**6 years** since 2020 (Treasury extended maturity somewhat). [Econofact](https://econofact.org/the-cost-of-financing-u-s-government-debt)
- **Refinancing / rollover risk:** ~**32%** of publicly-held marketable debt matures within **12 months** (Q2 FY2025); ~**61%** matures by end of 2028. Short profile means rate rises pass through fast. [Econofact — Interest Burden](https://econofact.org/explainer/the-interest-burden-of-the-federal-debt)

*Access date: 2026-07-23.*

## 4. Projections (CBO Long-Term Budget Outlook, March 2025 edition)

Source edition: **CBO, "The Long-Term Budget Outlook: 2025 to 2055," March 2025** ([cbo.gov/publication/61270](https://www.cbo.gov/publication/61270)); analysis via [CRFB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/debt-rises-175-gdp-under-cbos-long-term-outlook) and [American Action Forum](https://www.americanactionforum.org/insight/highlights-of-cbos-march-2025-long-term-budget-outlook/).

- **Debt held by the public:** ~**100% of GDP at end of FY2025** → new record **107% by 2029** → **~118% by 2035** → **~156% by 2055**. [CRFB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/debt-rises-175-gdp-under-cbos-long-term-outlook)
- 2055 debt is nearly double the pre-pandemic share and >3× the 50-year historical average (~50% of GDP). [AAF](https://www.americanactionforum.org/insight/highlights-of-cbos-march-2025-long-term-budget-outlook/)
- Under an **alternative scenario** (extending expiring policy), CBO shows debt rising toward **~175% of GDP** — hence the CRFB "Debt Rises to 175%" framing. [CRFB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/debt-rises-175-gdp-under-cbos-long-term-outlook)
- **Net interest trajectory:** rises steadily as a share of outlays and GDP; interest exceeds defense outlays every year 2025–2035 per CBO's Jan 2025 10-year baseline ([CBO — Budget & Economic Outlook 2025–2035](https://www.cbo.gov/publication/61172)).
- **Deficits:** ~6% of GDP now, projected toward ~7% and rising (Moody's cited a path toward ~9% by 2034).

*Access date: 2026-07-23. Note: verify against the latest CBO edition at publish time — CBO refreshes the long-term outlook roughly annually.*

| Agency | Date | Action | Stated reason |

First-ever US downgrade. [USAFacts](https://usafacts.org/articles/what-is-the-us-credit-rating/) · [House Budget Cmte](https://budget.house.gov/resources/staff-working-papers/us-debt-credit-rating-downgraded-only-second-time-in-nations-history) |
| **Fitch** | **Aug 1, 2023** | AAA → **AA+** | Expected fiscal deterioration over next 3 years, rising debt burden, and "erosion of governance" over 20 years (repeated debt-limit standoffs). [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2023/08/01/fitch-downgrades-us-long-term-ratings-to-aa-from-aaa.html) |
| **Moody's** | **May 16, 2025** | Aaa → **Aa1** (outlook stable) | Rising debt and interest costs; successive administrations/Congress failed to reverse large deficits; debt could reach ~134% of GDP by 2035 (from ~98% in 2024). Ended the last AAA the US had held (Moody's since 1917). [PGPF](https://www.pgpf.org/article/moodys-downgraded-its-us-credit-rating-and-warns-that-recent-policy-decisions-will-worsen-fiscal-outlook/) · [CNBC](https://www.cnbc.com/2025/05/16/moodys-downgrades-united-states-credit-rating-on-increase-in-government-debt.html) · [Moody's official](https://www.moodys.com/web/en/us/about-us/usrating.html) |

**Narrative hook:** After May 2025, **none of the three major agencies rates US debt at their top tier** — the first time in modern history. Moody's had maintained a perfect US rating since **1917**.

## 7. Debt Ceiling — Factual History & Current Status

- **Raised/revised ~78 times since 1960** — 49 under Republican presidents, 29 under Democratic. [NPR](https://www.npr.org/2023/04/29/1172894580/congress-has-revised-the-debt-ceiling-78-times-since-1960-a-financial-historian-) · [USAFacts](https://usafacts.org/articles/whats-the-history-of-debt-ceiling-increases-in-the-us/)
- **2023:** Fiscal Responsibility Act of 2023 (signed Jun 3, 2023) **suspended** the ceiling through **Jan 1, 2025**, paired with spending caps. [Congress.gov — H.R.3746](https://www.congress.gov/bill/118th-congress/house-bill/3746/summary/00) · [Crowell & Moring](https://www.crowell.com/en/insights/client-alerts/president-signs-compromise-fiscal-responsibility-act-debt-ceiling-suspension-paired-with-spending-caps-enforced-by-sequestration)
- **Jan 2, 2025:** ceiling **reinstated at ~$36.1 trillion** (the debt outstanding on that date); Treasury began "extraordinary measures." [PGPF](https://www.pgpf.org/article/the-debt-ceiling-will-be-reinstated-on-january-1-heres-whats-at-stake/) · [Congress.gov — Federal Debt and the Debt Limit in 2025](https://www.congress.gov/crs-product/IN12045)
- **Jul 4, 2025:** the **One Big Beautiful Bill Act (H.R. 1)** raised the statutory debt limit by **$5 trillion** — resolving the 2025 standoff. [U.S. News](https://money.usnews.com/money/personal-finance/family-finance/articles/trumps-one-big-beautiful-bill-just-raised-the-debt-ceiling-by-5-trillion-heres-why-that-matters-to-you) · [CRFB — What's in the OBBB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/whats-one-big-beautiful-bill-act)
- **Current status (mid-2026):** ceiling sits ~$5T above the early-2025 reinstatement level (~$41T range) following the July 2025 increase; no active default cliff. **Verify the exact statutory number and any 2026 action against [Treasury — Debt Limit](https://home.treasury.gov/policy-issues/financial-markets-financial-institutions-and-fiscal-service/debt-limit) before publishing.**

## 8. Surprising, Sourced Facts (for narrative color)

1. **You can literally donate to pay down the debt.** Treasury's Bureau of the Fiscal Service runs "Gifts to Reduce the Public Debt" — accepts money, savings bonds, even property. It has collected only **~$67.3 million since late 1996**; FY2022 was a mere **$180,310**. Donate via Pay.gov. [Treasury Fiscal Data — Gift Contributions](https://fiscaldata.treasury.gov/datasets/gift-contributions-reduce-debt-held-by-public/) · [Pay.gov form](https://www.pay.gov/public/form/start/23779454)
2. **The US has been completely debt-free for exactly two years — 1835 and 1836.** Andrew Jackson vetoed spending and sold western land to zero it out on Jan 1, 1835. It helped trigger the Panic of 1837 and a depression to 1844, which brought the debt right back. [HISTORY](https://www.history.com/this-day-in-history/january-1/andrew-jackson-national-debt-reaches-zero-dollars) · [NPR Planet Money](https://www.npr.org/sections/money/2011/04/15/135423586/when-the-u-s-paid-off-the-entire-national-debt-and-why-it-didnt-last)
3. **The Fed stopped sending the Treasury money — and is running a nine-figure "IOU to itself."** The Fed remitted **$76B in 2022** but remittances fell to **zero since September 2022**. In 2023 it posted a record **$114.3B operating loss** and a cumulative **$133.0B "deferred asset"** — future profits go to erasing that before any cash returns to Treasury. Lost remittances effectively widen the deficit. [St. Louis Fed](https://www.stlouisfed.org/on-the-economy/2023/nov/fed-remittances-treasury-explaining-deferred-asset) · [CRS](https://www.congress.gov/crs_external_products/IN/PDF/IN12081/IN12081.1.pdf)
4. **~$1 of every $5 in federal taxes now goes just to interest.** Interest ate **~18.5–19% of all federal revenue** in FY2025 — above the prior 1991 record — and interest is now a bigger line item than the entire defense budget and all of Medicare. [Econofact](https://econofact.org/explainer/the-interest-burden-of-the-federal-debt) · [CRFB](https://www.crfb.org/blogs/interest-costs-just-surpassed-defense-and-medicare)
5. **A third of the "national debt" is the government owing itself.** Of ~$38T gross, only debt *held by the public* (~$30T, ~100% of GDP) is borrowed on markets; ~$7T+ is **intragovernmental** — mostly the Social Security trust fund's **~$2.4T** in special-issue securities. As the trust funds draw down, that internal debt converts into real market borrowing. [CRFB — Gross Debt vs Debt Held by Public](https://www.crfb.org/papers/qa-gross-debt-versus-debt-held-public) · [PGPF](https://www.pgpf.org/article/the-federal-government-has-borrowed-trillions-but-who-owns-all-that-debt/)
6. **Debt grew ~$69,714 every second** over the trailing year (Joint Economic Committee estimate, Oct 2025). [PBS NewsHour](https://www.pbs.org/newshour/politics/u-s-hits-38-trillion-in-debt-after-the-fastest-accumulation-of-1-trillion-outside-of-the-pandemic)
