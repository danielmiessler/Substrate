# Research: US National Debt (July 2026)

Research project backing the `Data/US-National-Debt` dataset and https://usdebt.io.

**Question:** How big is US federal debt, who actually holds it, what does it cost, how did it get here, and where is it going — with every figure traceable to a primary source?

**Method:** Three parallel passes — (1) direct pulls from nine primary APIs/publications (Treasury Fiscal Data, TIC SLT, NY Fed SOMA, FRED), (2) holders research across CRS/Treasury/SSA/DoD/OPM/Fed Z.1/CFR, (3) history-milestones-interest-projections research across Treasury/CBO/IMF/rating agencies. Findings cross-checked against each other; conflicts documented rather than averaged.

**Contents:**
- `SOURCES.md` — source inventory with reliability notes
- `METHODOLOGY.md` — how figures were selected, reconciled, and bounded
- `findings/holders.md` — who holds the debt (full sourced breakdown)
- `findings/history-and-outlook.md` — history, trillion milestones, interest burden, projections, ratings

**Key findings (2026-07-23):** gross debt $39.66T (123% of GDP); ~75% held domestically; ~31% owed to the government itself (trust funds $7.79T + Fed $4.51T); Japan #1 foreign holder, UK #2 (passed China), China #3 at $659B TIC-basis (~1.7% of gross, custody-adjusted ~$1.8-1.9T); FY2025 net interest (~$970B) passed defense; all three rating agencies now below top grade.
